Sesame Chicken

Ingredients

For the Chicken

  • 1 1/2 lb (700 g) boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • Vegetable oil for frying (about 2–3 cups)

For the Sauce

  • 1/2 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/3 cup brown sugar (packed)
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar (or apple cider vinegar)
  • 1 tbsp hoisin sauce (optional, for depth)
  • 2 tsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 3 tbsp cold water (slurry)
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp grated fresh ginger

Garnish

  • 2 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • Cooked white rice or steamed vegetables, for serving

Instructions

  1. Pat the chicken pieces dry. In a large bowl combine flour, cornstarch, baking powder, salt, and pepper.
  2. Dip chicken pieces into the beaten eggs, then toss to coat in the flour mixture. Shake off excess.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep skillet or pot to 350°F (175°C). Fry chicken in batches until golden brown and cooked through, about 4–6 minutes per batch. Drain on a paper towel-lined plate.
  4. Meanwhile, in a small bowl whisk together soy sauce, water, brown sugar, rice vinegar, hoisin (if using), and sesame oil. Set aside.
  5. In a large skillet or wok over medium heat, add a tablespoon of oil and sauté garlic and ginger about 30 seconds until fragrant (do not burn).
  6. Pour the prepared sauce into the skillet and bring to a gentle simmer. Stir in the cornstarch slurry and cook until the sauce thickens and becomes glossy, about 1–2 minutes.
  7. Add the fried chicken to the skillet and toss to coat evenly with the sauce. Cook 1–2 minutes so the chicken absorbs the sauce.
  8. Transfer to a serving platter and sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds and sliced green onions. Serve immediately over steamed rice or with vegetables.

Notes & Tips

For extra crispiness, double-fry the chicken—fry once at a lower temperature to cook through, drain, then fry again quickly at higher heat to crisp. Adjust sugar and vinegar to taste for a sweeter or tangier sauce. Leftovers keep well and can be reheated in a hot oven to preserve crispness.
