Pastel de Nata (Portuguese Custard Tarts)

Ingredients

Dough

  • 1 sheet puff pastry (thawed)

Custard Filling

  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 6 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Zest of 1 lemon

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 250°C (480°F). Grease a 12-cup muffin tin.
  2. Roll out the puff pastry and cut into 12 equal pieces. Press each piece into the muffin cups, making sure the pastry comes up the sides.
  3. In a saucepan, whisk together milk and flour until smooth. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until thickened. Remove from heat.
  4. In another saucepan, combine sugar and water. Bring to a boil and cook until the syrup reaches thread stage (about 100°C/212°F). Remove from heat.
  5. Slowly whisk the hot syrup into the milk mixture.
  6. In a bowl, whisk egg yolks, vanilla, and lemon zest. Gradually add the milk-sugar mixture, whisking constantly.
  7. Strain the custard to remove any lumps.
  8. Pour the custard into the pastry-lined muffin cups, filling each about 3/4 full.
  9. Bake for 15–18 minutes, or until the custard is set and the tops are lightly browned.
  10. Cool slightly before removing from the tin. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Notes & Tips

Sprinkle with cinnamon or powdered sugar before serving for extra flavor.
